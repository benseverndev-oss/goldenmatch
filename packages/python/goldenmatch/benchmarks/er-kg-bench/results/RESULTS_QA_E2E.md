## Reading the table (commentary -- regenerating overwrites this file)

- **goldengraph matches the best GraphRAG engine at ~1/10th the cost.** Mean
  answer-match 0.174 vs LightRAG 0.173 (a tie within noise), at $0.31 vs $3.22 for
  the sweep. ms_graphrag (0.086) and graphiti (0.030) trail. This is a reversal of
  the first headline (goldengraph 0.101, behind LightRAG 0.185) after two fixes:
  adaptive retrieval depth + step-by-step synthesis.
- **goldengraph wins clean-entity and 2-hop; LightRAG wins high-ambiguity and
  1-hop.** goldengraph leads at amb 0.0/0.25 and on 2-hop (0.197 vs 0.141);
  LightRAG leads at amb >= 0.5 and on 1-hop.
- **Honest caveat on the thesis:** the program predicted ER would make goldengraph
  decay *slower* under ambiguity. It does the opposite -- goldengraph decays faster
  (0.416 -> 0.040) than LightRAG (0.285 -> 0.094). goldengraph wins on clean-data
  strength + multi-hop + cost, NOT ambiguity-resilience. The citable claim is
  "matches the best at 1/10th the cost," not "ER moat beats them under noise."
- ms_graphrag cost reads $0 (config-driven LLM, unmetered -- approximate, not free).
  Engineered corpus is synthetic; absolute numbers are low by construction.
