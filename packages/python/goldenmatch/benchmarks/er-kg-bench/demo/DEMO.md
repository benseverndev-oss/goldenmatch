## Under-merge: a fragmented entity gives an incomplete answer

The corpus entity `Q7817` has 3 surface forms: `World Health Organisation`, `World Health Organization`, `World Health Organization (WHO)`.

**Before — exact-match KG (GraphRAG / LightRAG / Cognee / mem0 family).** These resolve only byte-identical names, so each surface form becomes its own node. Agent question: *"How many organizations are in memory, and what are World Health Organization's known names?"*

> 3 organizations (the entity is stored 3 times); names reachable from "World Health Organization": `World Health Organization`.

That is the failure ER-KG-Bench measures in aggregate: the exact-match family scores **F1 0.066** on real surface variation.

**After — zero-config `goldenmatch.dedupe_df` (`auto+fields`).** Multi-field ER (name + type + context) unifies the surface forms into one entity.

> 1 organization; names reachable from "World Health Organization": `World Health Organisation`, `World Health Organization`, `World Health Organization (WHO)`.

Same documents, same query. The answer goes from a duplicated, half-blind entity to one complete entity. Behavior is the harness's own adapters over the real corpus; the only construction is treating each surface form as one ingested mention, which is exactly what real KG/agent-memory ingestion does.
