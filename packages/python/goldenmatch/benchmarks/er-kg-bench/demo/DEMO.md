## Under-merge: a fragmented entity gives an incomplete answer

The corpus entity `Q7184` has 4 surface forms: `NATO`, `NATO Alliance`, `North Atlantic Alliance`, `North Atlantic Treaty Organisation`.

**Before — exact-match KG (GraphRAG / LightRAG / Cognee / mem0 family).** These resolve only byte-identical names, so each surface form becomes its own node. Agent question: *"How many organizations are in memory, and what are NATO's known names?"*

> 4 organizations (the entity is stored 4 times); names reachable from "NATO": `NATO`.

That is the failure ER-KG-Bench measures in aggregate: the exact-match family scores **F1 0.066** on real surface variation.

**After — zero-config `goldenmatch.dedupe_df` (`auto+fields`).** Multi-field ER (name + type + context) unifies the surface forms into one entity.

> 1 organization; names reachable from "NATO": `NATO`, `NATO Alliance`, `North Atlantic Alliance`, `North Atlantic Treaty Organisation`.

Same documents, same query. The answer goes from a duplicated, half-blind entity to one complete entity. Behavior is the harness's own adapters over the real corpus; the only construction is treating each surface form as one ingested mention, which is exactly what real KG/agent-memory ingestion does.
